Pioneering women

Hertha Ayrton
the first woman to win a prize from the Royal Society, 1906 winner of the Hughes Medal for her work on electric arcs and ripples in sand and water

Josephine Baker
the first Black woman to star in a major motion picture, she refused to perform for segregated audiences in the United States

Eugénie Brazier
the first chef to attain a total of six Michelin stars in 1933, first woman to earn three. It took 65 years for another chef to be awarded six stars

Émilie du Châtelet
first proposed the law of conservation of energy - that energy can neither be created nor destroyed - in her 1700s translation of Newton's Principia (still in use in French today)
